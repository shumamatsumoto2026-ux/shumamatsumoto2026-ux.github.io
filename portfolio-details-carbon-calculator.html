Mastercard Carbon Calculator

Project Overview

The Carbon Calculator is a cornerstone of Mastercard's commitment to sustainability and the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). This mobile-first application allows cardholders to monitor the estimated carbon footprint of their purchases in real-time. By synchronizing transaction data with environmental impact metrics, the app provides users with immediate feedback on their CO2 emissions and actionable advice on how to reduce their environmental footprint. I was responsible for bringing the high-fidelity prototypes to life, ensuring a seamless and educational user journey.

  • Website: https://www.mastercard.com/global/en/for-the-world/planet/priceless-planet/carbon-calculator.html
My Role & Responsibilities

As the Senior Frontend Developer and UI Lead, I translated complex environmental data into an intuitive mobile experience:

  • Frontend Architecture: I developed the entire frontend of the application using React, ensuring the app was lightweight, fast, and responsive.
  • UI/UX Implementation: I transformed complex InVision prototypes and Sketch designs into Fimga.
  • SDG Integration: I worked closely with product leads to ensure the interface effectively communicated the "Sustainable Development Goals" mission, making data about CO2 emissions easy for the average consumer to understand.
  • Platform Maintenance & Education: Following the initial launch, I served as a subject matter expert, training the maintenance team on the codebase to ensure the platform could be easily updated as new environmental data became available.
Technical Skills Applied

This project highlighted my transition into modern JavaScript frameworks and mobile-first development:

  • Frontend Frameworks: Advanced React (developing functional components and managing state) and JavaScript.
  • Web Technologies: Expert-level HTML5 and CSS3 specifically optimized for mobile application environments.
  • Design-to-Code Tools: Expert workflow management using Sketch, InVision, and Figma.
  • Sustainability UX: Applying user-centric design principles to environmental data, ensuring "information density" did not overwhelm the user experience.
