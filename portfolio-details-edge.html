EDGE (Experience Design Guides & Enablement)

Project Overview

The EDGE project was a major strategic rebranding and platform evolution (formerly Design Standards for Products) aimed at solving a critical business challenge: how to help global partners implement Mastercard products faster and with higher quality.

While Mastercard provides powerful APIs, partners often lacked the UX/UI resources to build effective end-customer experiences. EDGE serves as a digital "Experience Design Guide" embedded within the Mastercard Developers platform. It provides partners with full white-label Figma user flows and best-practice documentation, ensuring that every partner-built application meets the "Mastercard-level" standard for consistency, adoption, and speed-to-market.

My Role & Responsibilities

As a Senior Specialist in Visual Design, I served as the technical lead and platform owner for the EDGE digital headquarters. My responsibilities included:

  • Platform Development & Maintenance: I built and managed the EDGE CX&D Guild Hubsite, transforming it from a placeholder into a comprehensive self-service resource center for internal and external stakeholders.
  • Figma Ecosystem Management: I was granted editing rights to the core EDGE Figma files to manage the integration of new branding, logos, and user experience flows for products.
  • DesignOps & Standardization: I collaborated with CX Leads to integrate EDGE into the Mastercard Studio Framework. I ensured the guide served as a key metric during the "Market Test" phase to de-risk product launches.
  • Cross-Functional Collaboration: I acted as the primary connection point between Product, Technology, and Sales, ensuring that all "Quick Links" (Roadmaps, Plan documents, and FAQs) were organized for maximum utility.
Technical Skills Applied

This project showcased my ability to merge sophisticated design tools with technical platform management:

  • Design Tools: Advanced Figma (managing shared libraries and user flows), InVision, and Sketch.
  • Platform Engineering: SharePoint Online (Architecture and Site Development), HTML5/CSS3 (for customized web elements)..
  • UX & Strategy: Information Architecture (IA), Design Operations (DesignOps), and CX Metrics development.
