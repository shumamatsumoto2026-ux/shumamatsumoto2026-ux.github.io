POGS (Product Onboarding Gold Standards) Platform Evolution

Project Overview

In 2024, POGS transitioned from a recommended design guideline into a formal Craig Vosburg-level mandate. Every market-facing product at Mastercard is now measured against the POGS scorecard, which directly influences leadership bonus calculations.

The primary business objective of POGS is a 20% reduction in Total Time to Implementation (TTI). To support this, I led the evolution of the POGS digital platform, transforming it into a high-scale, self-service hub. This hub enables product teams to identify delivery improvements, access case studies, and integrate gold-standard onboarding practices into their product roadmaps.

My Role & Responsibilities

As the Senior Specialist for Visual Design and Platform Lead, I was responsible for the structural and visual integrity of the POGS ecosystem:

  • Information Architecture (IA) Evolution: Partnered with CX Leads to redesign the site's IA, ensuring the platform could scale to host an influx of case studies, "helper" content, and technical documentation without increasing user friction.
  • Component-Based Design Strategy: Developed new, reusable article formats and UI components for case studies. This ensured that as the program grew, the content remained consistent and professional.
  • Cross-Metric Alignment: Worked closely with team leads (Tim Schmidt and Lucy Woodham) to ensure the POGS "look and feel" remained cohesive with other CX metrics like CDS and DQS, allowing for a unified suite of offerings.
  • Feature Integration: Successfully implemented an "Ask the Expert" scheduling capability, bridging the gap between static self-service content and live consultation with CX practitioners.
Technical Skills Applied

This project required a combination of strategic UX thinking and advanced platform management:

  • UI/UX Design: Expert use of Figma to lead the "innovative design" refresh and create scalable content templates.
  • Platform Development: Advanced SharePoint Online architecture and custom web part configuration.
  • Design Operations (DesignOps): Establishing a "common framework" for experience KPIs and tracking desired outcomes across a global portfolio.
  • Business Strategy: Integrating design deliverables with business KPIs such as Total Time to Implementation (TTI) and corporate scorecard requirements.
