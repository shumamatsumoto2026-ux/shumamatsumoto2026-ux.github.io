Global Newsletter Centralization & Design System

Project Overview

Mastercard's internal teams across the globe often operated in silos, resulting in a high volume of fragmented newsletters with inconsistent branding and varying quality. I spearheaded an initiative to centralize and optimize these internal communications.

I transformed the "Monthly Global Newsletter" from a manual design task into a scalable internal design system. This project was recognized with the H.E.A.R.T. Award, honoring my commitment to the "Mastercard Way" by delivering a best-in-class communication tool that increased global employee engagement and streamlined cross-functional updates.

My Role & Responsibilities

As the Senior Lead for Digital Communication Design, I acted as a "Change Agent" to improve how our guild communicates:

  • Award-Winning Creative Direction: I led the end-to-end design and delivery of the global newsletter, ensuring a premium visual experience that reflected Mastercard's brand identity.
  • Internal Creation Centralization: I developed a framework that allowed the CX&D Guild, AP Chapter, and Global Product teams to generate high-quality content using a unified source of truth, eliminating design fragmentation.
  • DesignOps Implementation: I partnered with leadership on "Unlocked" projects to create a structured intake process, ensuring that newsletter production was predictable, scalable, and efficient.
  • Template Engineering: I built a library of modular, responsive HTML templates that ensured consistent rendering across all internal platforms (Outlook, Web, and Mobile).
Technical Skills Applied

This project required a unique blend of "technical wizardry" and strategic operational thinking:

  • Frontend Engineering: Advanced HTML5 and CSS3 specialized for Email Development (EDM) to ensure cross-client compatibility.
  • UI/UX & Visual Design: Expert use of Figma and Sketch to create a modular component library for internal communications.
  • Design Systems & Ops: Establishing a centralized "News Center" workflow and standardized templates for the wider organization.
  • Accessibility & Governance: Integrating Mastercard Content Guidelines to ensure all communications were inclusive and met corporate standards.
